Caenorhabiditis elegans

The nematode C. elegans is a tiny worm that keeps many scientist around the world busy. The work with the worm was honored by 2 nobelprizes
Sidney Brenner introduced this nematode as model organism to study neuronal networks and
development over 50 years ago. The worm helped to make many important
discoveries as for example a first full description of a neuronal network, lifespan prolonged genes, and RNAi.

Drosophila melangolaster

Research with the fruit fly started with Charles Woodworth. It was the first genetic model organism and was honored with 8 nobelprizes.
Important work was done by Christina Nuesslein Vollhard and collegues in the 80s. She discovered important developmental genes and described their function

Zebrafish

George Streisinger and collegues started in the 1979s to use D. rerio in their labs.
It is a tiny fresh water fish with an incredible regenerative abilities. It is finding cures for spinal cord injuries.
