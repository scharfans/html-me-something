Caenorhabiditis elegans

The nematode C. elegans is a tiny worm that keeps many scientist around the world busy.
Sidney Brenner introduced this nematode as model organism to study neuronal networks and
development over 50 years ago. The worm helped to make many important
discoveries as for example a first full description of a neuronal network, lifespan prolonged genes, and RNAi.
